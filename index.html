Recovery Protocol
Daily · Injury-specific · Between heats · Sleep

Knee — Every Session

  • Ice immediately post session — 15 min wrapped
  • Leg elevation 20 min post training
  • No back-to-back leg-heavy days in weeks 1–2
  • VMO terminal knee extensions 3x15 daily
  • Step-ups on low box — 3x10 each leg, controlled
  • Sharp pain 4/10+ = stop that movement immediately
  • Any swelling = report same day, do not train through it

Cervical — Daily

  • Chin tucks x10 morning and night every day
  • Upper trap stretch — 30 sec each side
  • Levator scapulae stretch — 30 sec each side
  • No heavy load behind or above neck
  • Neutral neck pillow — no forward head flexion sleeping
  • Limit looking-down phone use this entire block
  • STOP immediately if arm or hand tingling appears

Sleep Protocol

  • Minimum 8 hrs · Target 9 hrs during training weeks
  • Sleep before midnight — GH peaks 10pm–2am
  • Room temperature 18–20°C
  • No screens 45 min before bed
  • Magnesium glycinate 300mg every night
  • Race eve: if anxious, don't force sleep — bank it 2 nights prior
  • Consistent wake time even on rest days

Active Recovery Tools

  • Cold shower 2–3 min post hard sessions
  • Foam roll: quads, IT band, thoracic spine — 10 min
  • Compression on legs 2 hrs after hard session
  • 20 min walk every rest day — no full sedentary rest
  • Epsom salt bath on very sore days — 20 min

Between Heats — Race Day

  • Sit down immediately — off your feet
  • Fast carbs within 10 min — banana, dates, sports drink
  • Sip electrolytes every few minutes
  • Light walk at 15 min mark — don't seize up
  • Change socks if possible
  • Mental reset — heat 2 is a new race
  • 20–25 min gap between heats minimum
  • Ice knee briefly if it's complaining

Weekly Recovery Rules

  • Weeks 1–2: Never train 3 days in a row
  • Week 3: Max 2 consecutive days then mandatory rest
  • Week 4: More rest than training
  • Energy crash mid-week = take extra rest day. Don't push.
  • Soreness is okay. Joint pain is not. Know the difference.
Strength Training
4-week block · Knee + cervical modified · 2 sessions/week
Why Strength in This BlockHe has 40kg muscle mass at 90kg — undertrained relative to bodyweight. Sled push, sandbag lunge, and farmer carry all demand muscular strength under fatigue. Without strength work, he will break down at stations 6–9 in the double. Strength sessions are programmed on non-cardio days.
Rules for His ConditionsNo heavy barbell back squat — knee + cervical risk. No overhead pressing heavy — cervical. No loaded good mornings or behind-neck anything. All loads moderate — endurance rep ranges, not max strength. Stop any exercise if knee hits 4/10 pain or arm tingling starts.
Strength Session Structure

Every Strength Session Format

  • Warm up: 8 min — bike or row easy + dynamic mobility
  • VMO activation: Terminal knee extensions 2x15 each leg
  • Chin tuck activation: 10 reps before any loaded carry
  • Main work: 4 exercises — 3 sets each
  • Cool down: 10 min stretch — quads, hips, upper trap
  • Ice knee after every session — 15 min

Knee Protocol

Status: 80% recovered

Highest risk: Sled Push, Sandbag Lunge, Running. Any knee-loaded exercise under fatigue must be monitored.

Every session: Ice 15 min immediately after. Leg elevation 20 min. VMO extensions 3x15 daily.

Stop signs: Sharp pain 4/10+, swelling, locking, clicking with pain. Stop the movement and report same day.

Race day: Step-back burpee available. Reduce sled load if needed. Knee over toe always on lunges.

Cervical Protocol

Status: Active issue — managed carefully

Highest risk: Farmer Carry, Ski Erg, Sandbag on shoulders. Loaded carry or pull can compress cervical spine if form breaks.

Daily non-negotiables: Chin tucks x10 morning and night. Upper trap stretch 30 sec each side.

Hard stop: Tingling or numbness in arms or fingers during any carry = stop immediately. Nerve involvement. Do not train through this.

Sleep: Neutral neck pillow. No forward head position while sleeping.

Race Goal

Goal: Safe Finish — Both Heats

4 weeks prep, 80% knee, active cervical issue. The goal is not time. The goal is crossing both finish lines healthy and intact.

Heat 1: 70% effort. Survive smart. Protect high-risk stations.

Heat 2: 80% effort. Pacing heat 1 correctly makes heat 2 possible.

What winning looks like: He finishes. He doesn't re-injure the knee. He walks away able to train again the following week.
